Info:  
In a laser harp, laser beams replace traditional strings. The beams are emitted by lasers and detected by the LDRs. Each beam corresponds to a specific musical note. When a player interrupts a beam with their hand, the sensor detects the drop in light intensity and sends a signal to the circuit. This signal is the processed to generate a musical tone corresponding to the interrupted beam. By moving their hands through the beams, the player "plays" the harp, with each beam acting as a unique string producing different notes.
